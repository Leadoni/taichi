10 Chair Tai Chi Exercises

Gentle guided videos you can do from a chair — for balance, mobility & calm. Just press play and follow along.
Beginner

Gentle starting points — find your posture and breath.

01

Seated chest openers

1 min · follow along
Beginner
  1. Sit comfortably in a chair with your feet flat on the floor.
  2. Inhale deeply and extend your arms out to the sides at shoulder height, opening your chest. Hold this position and take deep breaths, feeling the stretch across your chest and shoulders.
02

Seated bicep curls

1 min · follow along
Beginner
  1. Sit tall on a chair with your feet flat on the ground hip-width apart.
  2. Make fists with your hands by your sides, palms facing each other.
  3. Curl your forearms towards your shoulders, engaging your biceps. If using weights, hold them in each hand.
  4. Lower your arms back to your sides with control and repeat.
03

Seated revolved chair pose

2 min · follow along
Beginner
  1. Sit tall on a chair with your feet flat on the ground together or hip-width apart.
  2. Inhale, extend your arms overhead with your palms facing each other.
  3. As you exhale, bring your hands together at heart center, then twist your torso to the left, hooking your right elbow outside your left thigh. Keep your spine long and your chest open as you press your palms together, deepening the twist with each exhale. Hold the pose, maintaining core and leg strength.
Intermediate

A little more movement, balance and reach.

04

Seated side bend (right)

1 min · follow along
Intermediate
  1. Sit comfortably in a chair with your feet flat on the floor.
  2. Inhale deeply and lift your right arm overhead.
  3. Exhale slowly and reach to the left side, feeling a stretch along the right side of your body. Hold this position and take deep breaths.
05

Seated pigeon pose (left)

1 min · follow along
Intermediate
  1. Sit comfortably in a chair with your feet flat on the floor and your hands resting on your thighs.
  2. Inhale deeply, lengthening your spine and engaging your core.
  3. Cross your left ankle over your right knee, flexing your left foot. Hold for a gentle stretch in your left hip and thigh, adjusting your knee position as needed.
06

Seated knee to elbows

1 min · follow along
Intermediate
  1. Sit on the edge of a sturdy chair with your feet flat on the floor.
  2. Place your hands behind your head, elbows out to the sides.
  3. Inhale deeply and exhale as you crunch your left elbow towards your right knee, engaging your oblique muscles.
  4. Come back to the starting position and repeat, alternating sides each time.
07

Seated arm circles (backward)

1 min · follow along
Intermediate
  1. Sit tall on a chair with your feet flat on the ground, hip-width apart.
  2. Extend your arms out to the sides at shoulder height, palms facing down.
  3. Begin making circles with your arms going in a backward direction. Keep your core engaged and your spine tall throughout the movement.
Advanced

Stronger work for legs, core and spine.

08

Chair squats

1 min · follow along
Advanced
  1. Stand with your feet hip-width apart in front of a chair.
  2. Bend your knees and lower your hips down until your hips reach the chair without completely sitting down.
  3. Push through your heels to return to the standing position and repeat, maintaining a good position throughout with your shoulders rolled back and your core engaged.
09

Seated cat cow

1 min · follow along
Advanced
  1. Sit comfortably in a chair with your feet flat on the floor.
  2. Inhale deeply, arch your lower back and lift your chest into cow pose.
  3. Exhale slowly, round your spine from your lower back to your shoulders, tucking your chin in for cat pose.
  4. Flow smoothly between these poses, syncing your movements with your breath.
10

Chair supported quad stretch (right)

1 min · follow along
Advanced
  1. Stand next to a chair with your left hand resting on the back for support.
  2. Bend your right knee and bring your right heel towards your glutes.
  3. Reach back with your right hand to grasp your right ankle, gently pulling it closer while keeping your knees close together. Engage your core and maintain a straight posture. Hold, feeling a stretch in your right quad.
